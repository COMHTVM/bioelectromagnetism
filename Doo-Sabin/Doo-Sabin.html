Doo-Sabin Subdivision

In the late seventies, D.Doo and M.A.Sabin proposed an algorithm to define B-Spline surfaces over topologically arbitrary polygon meshes. The idea behind it is quite simple, and can be refered to as "corner- and edgecutting". Nowadays, there exist several variants of this algorithm, including weighted versions that use additional parameters to adjust details of the resulting shape. Here is one generic Doo-Sabin step.

It can be shown, that the sequence of shapes obtained by recursive subdivision converges to a biquadratic B-Spline surface, except perhaps in the neighbourhood of a finite number of points, where it converges to a C^1 surface.
